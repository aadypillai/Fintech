Quantitative Investment Committee

Quant investment is a combination of fundamental and technical analysis of securities, often utilizing machine learning and reinforcement learning concepts to make informed decisions. This committee will train members who have a basic background in programming, statistics, and linear algebra to be able to utilize ML and its applications in quantitative investment. Applicants are not expected to have an ML background, but must have experience with the following:

- Linear algebra (MATH 54, EE16A or equivalent)

- Understanding of gradients and partial derivatives (MATH 53 would work)

- Basic statistics (DATA 8 is a plus)

- Jupyter Notebooks (nice to have)

Our committee members should be comfortable implementing their own trading algorithms and strategies by the end of our 6-week training program in ML/RL in addition to its applications to finance. Through this, we hope that CMs can learn to analytically interpret different forms of investments and financial securities through the use of mathematics and computer science. Furthermore, we hope to encourage a collaborative environment both within the quantitative investment team and with members in other committees through the sharing of short-term and long-term investment strategies and methods of determining risk for financial assets.

Tentative Schedule

Module One -- Overview of Libra
Module Two -- Cryptocurrency exchange methods and regulations
Module Three -- Fundamentals of Blockchain Programming
Module Four -- Cryptocurrency Project
